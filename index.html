This gal used to be a regular student before she got bit. She used to love eating all kinds of food, but all she wants now is some human brains to eat. Her strength is below average - so be extra careful around humans - but her life bar decreases slower than most zombies.

The Situation:

A cure to zombie-ism has been found! Lucky for you, the zombie-curing-Science-Lab is located only several miles away from where you are. To get a proper vaccine, you must bring with you a collection of zombie-strain fungi, a minimum of 6. There are 10 hidden all over the city and you must find them on the way to the lab.

However, you still have to deal with that constant hunger for human brains and whatnot. You want to make it to that lab to turn back into a human, but you definitely can't make it on an empty stomach. If you don't feed yourself every so often, you WILL die (deader than you already are). Watch out for the life bar! If it hits zero, it's over. So that means if you run into some humans... you do what you gotta do.

You may also pick up objects along the way to help you on your quest. But some objects are not as helpful as you think...

Good luck!



This guy used to be an actor before he got bit. Now his only joy in "life" is slurping fresh brains out of human heads. His strength is above average, but his life bar decreases a bit faster and needs feeding more often.

Your Goal:

Your goal is to get zombie to the Science Lab ALIVE (as alive as can be anyway). Make sure to keep zombie well-fed... do whatever it takes. And be sure to judge a situation wisely, as it could mean life or death (for the most part).

The Science Lab is 15 blocks straight down this street from where you are. However, you are encouraged to explore the streets to the right and left, to see if there are any zombie-strain fungus or useful objects to pick up, or... brains to munch on. Hover over the screen to detect for hidden items or humans to attack and click to take action. Choose your path by clicking the corresponding arrows (straight, left, or right).

Once you pass a block (straight arrow), you can no longer turn back! Your zombie brain just can't comprehend that much.
